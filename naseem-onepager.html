The Problem

In the Gulf, the buildings that waste the most are the ones cooled around the clock for people who are only there part of the day — mosques, schools, government halls, event venues. Their air conditioners run independently, on fixed schedules, chilling empty volumes for hours.

Today's options don't solve it. Smart thermostats fix one unit at a time and react after the room is already wrong. Building management systems are expensive, custom, and still don't coordinate units toward a shared comfort-and-energy goal.

The Product — how Naseem works
01 Sense

Read the building

A small edge device reads temperature, humidity, and occupancy across zones. It retrofits onto existing AC — no rip-and-replace.

02 Coordinate

One team, one goal

Multi-agent AI treats each unit as a player on one team — forecasting arrivals, pre-cooling only the zones that need it, balancing comfort, energy, and peak load together.

03 Act

Coordinated set-points

Units receive real-time set-points. Comfort stays inside guaranteed bounds; everything else is optimized away.

Why it's different

Thermostats and building management systems control units in isolation and react after the fact. Naseem coordinates the whole building toward one goal, predicts occupancy instead of reacting, and is grid-aware — shifting load away from costly peak hours.

The moat — provably-safe AI

Verified, not just trained

Naseem is the first building-control AI whose decisions are formally verified — mathematically proven never to breach comfort or safety limits. For a facility manager, that removes the core fear of handing control to an AI: a defensible, hard-to-copy edge built on the founder's verification research.
